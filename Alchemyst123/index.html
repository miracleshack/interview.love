The spotlight's on Alchemyst123:

What's your most bizarre life experience?

These questions? Kind of intense. I'd have to think about it. Once I was in a ride with an uber, and this guy starts going off about the Jesuits, Black Jesus, and how Trump will save the world by killing the Bidens. I also once went door to door selling things for a breast cancer charity that helped families suffering by it. I went to one house, it was worn down, the inside was worn down. There was a woman who answered, with sores and lessions with a lazy eye, and blood all over the place. I asked if she's okay... if there was someone I could call. She said "no, no, thank you." There was this man standing around from a construction company or something. I asked what was going on, he said something like: "I don't know what you're talking about, no one's lived there for years. It's been foreclosed to the bank." There was another time I was doing something for a Red Cross program that helped out families in need of providing for their kids. This man who was literally scratching himself all over, completely drugged comes up to me and says: "I hate it when parents spend all there money on drugs and don't spend enough to even feed there kids." I was like [to myself]: "Are you one of them?" Lastly, I was in Vietnam... heavy Vietnam traffic isn't unusal. There was a woman on the floor from a crash with no covering over her, and the responding officers just left her there while waving people to go on. The squatter lady? It still creeps me out, as if I was pitching sales to a ghost. She was very kind.
